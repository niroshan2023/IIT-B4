Week 1: Introduction to Submarine Design

In the first week, we will cover the basics of submarine design, including the history of submarines, key principles, and the different types of submarines. Students will gain a solid foundation in submarine construction and operation.

Week 2: Materials and Construction

This week, we'll dive into the materials used in submarine construction and the techniques involved in building a submarine. Topics will include pressure hulls, ballast systems, and various engineering considerations for submersible vessels.

Week 3: Propulsion Systems

Week 3 will focus on propulsion systems for submarines. Students will learn about different types of propulsion, such as nuclear, diesel-electric, and air-independent propulsion. We'll also discuss the pros and cons of each system.

Week 4: Submarine Operations and Safety

During this week, we'll explore various submarine operations and safety measures. Topics include underwater navigation, communication, life support systems, and emergency procedures. Students will gain insights into the challenges of operating a submarine and ensuring the well-being of the crew during extended underwater missions.

Final Project: Submarine Design and Presentation

During the final week, students will work on a group project to design a hypothetical submarine for a specific mission. Each group will present their design to the class, explaining the rationale behind their choices.

Prerequisites

Optional prerequisites for students who want to further enhance their understanding:

    •A basic understanding of engineering principles
    •amiliarity with physics and mathematics
    •An interest in marine technology and exploration
Course Duration

The course will run for a total of 4 weeks, with one 2-hour session per week.

Certification

Students who successfully complete the course will receive a certificate of completion in Submarine Building.
